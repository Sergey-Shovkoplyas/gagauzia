Supporting Inclusive Dialogue and Strengthening Capacities for a Better Functioning Gagauz Autonomy in Moldova” is a three-year project facilitated by CMI and funded by Sweden. The project also partners with EURAC Research in Italy, and CONTACT Center in Moldova.

About CMI:
CMI is an independent Finnish organization that works to prevent and resolve conflicts through Nobel Peace laureate and former President of Finland Martti Ahtisaari founded CMI in 2000. Since then, the organization has grown to become one of the leaders in its field. CMI works closely with all conflicting parties in some of the world’s most intractable conflicts to forge lasting peace through informal dialogue and mediation.
About Eurac Research:
Since 1992, the Institute for Comparative Federalism and the Institute for Minority Rights of South Tyrol, Italy, do basic and applied research on federal, regional and local issues, and in the fields of minority rights and territorial autonomies. The team, composed of comparative constitutional lawyers and political scientists, looks at how constitutionalism and transnational law affects the governance of societal and territorial pluralism.
About CONTACT Centre:
a non-profit independent non-governmental organisation. Since 1995, the Centre serves as resource, analysis, knowledge and skills for civil society leaders from Moldova. CONTACT Centre take an active part in the development of a democratic society in Moldova by strengthening the potential of civil society, encouraging civic initiative and promoting the good governance principles.
About Sweden:
Sweden has a long-term commitment for continued support to Eastern Partnership 2014-2020. Key priority areas include strengthened democracy, greater respect for human rights, a more fully developed state under the rule of law, and gender inclusion.
